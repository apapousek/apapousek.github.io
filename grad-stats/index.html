The university is losing graduate students, but those who remain are pulling their weight

by Tony Papousek

A Falling Student Body

Chancellor Harvey Perlman said that he wanted UNL to have 30,000 students enrolled by 2020. So far, it hasn't been working out too well.

A large part of the student body is composed of graduate students.

According to statistics from UNL's Office of Institutional Research and Planning, the number of graduate students has been dropping sharply since 2011.

During the same 4 year time period, the number of graduate research assistants fluctuated in almost the exact same pattern. That shouldn't be too much of a surprise, but there's something else going on.

The interesting part?

The number of gratuate teaching assistants rose significantly in the past 4 years.





This means that graduate students are more likely to become teaching assistants than they ever have in the past 10 years.

Even though the student enrollment at Lincoln is at a 32-year high, the number of grad students has been dropping quickly.

If the numbers are correct, current graduate students are getting down to work to make up the difference.
